INFORMATION FOR WALMART SUPPLY CHAIN EMPLOYEES

Did you receive a mailer recently (around mid-June) about a lawsuit Flores v. Walmart?

Walmart settled lawsuits with Manuel Flores and others to settle all labor law claims any of its California warehouse workers might have – specifically YOU.1

We are not those lawyers. We are the lawyers who went to trial against Walmart in April 2019 and WON! We got a verdict for over $6 million for 4,840 workers. Walmart still is trying to avoid paying, but we’ve already won on appeal and Walmart will have to pay at least the $6 million. We are still fighting hard for more!

We represent the class in Hamilton, Hernnadez v. Wal-Mart Stores, Inc., Wal-Mart Associates, Inc., Case No. 5:17-cv-01415 AB (KKx). As you might know, we went to trial in April 2019 and won $6 million for all the workers at the facility at 6750 Kimball Avenue from when it opened until August 21, 2018.2 The jury gave about 90% what we wanted for meals, which means for everyone that worked there during that time, they get 90% of their hourly pay for every shift over 6 hours. Someone who was paid $15 an hour is entitled to get $13.50 for almost every day they worked (minus attorneys’ fees and costs)

You haven’t been paid in Hamilton v. Walmart because Walmart is still fighting and we are still fighting for more than $6 million. Walmart has told us they have the right to settle with Flores in order to avoid paying Hamilton and the workers at 6750 Kimball Avenue. We think you should know about this.

In Flores, people get about $11.28 a week for all your wage and hour claims. That’s compared to the Hamilton verdict, which was about that per shift for just the meal breaks.

We have already filed papers with the court in Flores and expressed these issues. If you’d like to add your voice, you have two options.

WHAT CAN YOU DO?

You basically have two options. You can opt-out of the Flores settlement, keep your rights to sue Walmart for more money. Or you can object (complain) about the Flores settlement and ask the judge to reject it and get your share (about $11.28 a week, the actual amount is in your Flores notice). We have prepared an automated form for you to fill out and we will send it in for you.3

You can choose one of the two options, not both. People who are part of the settlement can complain about it, people who choose to opt out cannot.
